01 — The problem

Your company has two org charts. Only one is governed.

Workday knows your employees. Entra knows your identities. But the agent workforce growing underneath them — scattered across builder tools and notebooks — has no lineage, no sponsor, and no lifecycle tied to the people who created it.

/ 01

Hidden agent workforce

Agents multiply across Copilot Studio, LangGraph, internal builders, MCP servers, notebooks, and SaaS automations. IAM never sees most of them.

/ 02

No human accountability

When an agent touches revenue or finance data, teams can't answer who owns it, who approved it, and why it was built in the first place.

/ 03

Lifecycle gaps

When employees leave or change roles, their agents remain active, over-permissioned, or orphaned. HR events don't cascade to the agent graph.

02 — The thesis

Govern agents like the humans who created them.

Pedigree inherits your human org chart and extends it with agent lineage — so every autonomous action has a human you can name.

08 — Objections, handled

Common questions.

Is this replacing Entra, Okta, Workday, or ServiceNow? +
No. Pedigree sits above and between them as the lineage and evidence layer for AI agents. It connects human ownership, app-owner approvals, permission boundaries, and HR lifecycle events — it doesn't replace the systems of record.
What about agents that need to outlive their creator? +
Pedigree supports org-owned agents with explicit exception workflows, sponsor reassignment, and audit evidence. Human-parented is the default; exceptions are visible and reviewable.
Do we need runtime enforcement on day one? +
No. Most teams start with read-only lineage, risk findings, and audit evidence. Runtime enforcement becomes much easier once the ownership graph is trusted.
What is the first step? +
Run an Agent Lineage Assessment. Start with known agents, map ownership, identify risk, and build the first governance baseline. You can see the interactive demo to understand the shape of the deliverable.
